Research and Professional Practice

Module completed in October to December 2025, focused on academic research, professional ethics, reflective practice and research methods in computing.

Module Overview

This module introduced the foundations of research and professional practice in computing. It developed my understanding of research design, literature searching, ethical considerations, data protection, professional conduct and reflective development.

My research topic focused on synthetic data generation and machine learning for insider threat detection in cloud environments. This allowed me to connect academic research methods with my wider interests in cybersecurity, cloud computing and site reliability engineering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the research process, including research questions, literature reviews and methodology design.
  • Apply ethical principles in the planning, execution and reporting of research.
  • Use appropriate data collection and analysis methods to address research questions.
  • Communicate research findings effectively through structured academic writing.
  • Reflect on professional development and identify future growth opportunities.

Artefact 1: Individual Research Proposal

The mid-module assignment was a 1,000-word individual research proposal on synthetic data generation and machine learning for insider threat detection in cloud environments.

  • Due date: 10 November 2025
  • Weighting: 20%
  • Grade: 70% - Distinction
  • Topic: Synthetic data generation for insider threat detection in cloud environments

The proposal included a literature search, research aim and objectives, research question, methodology discussion, ethical considerations and potential contributions to the field.

Feedback highlighted a strong introduction, sound aims and objectives, good structure and evidence of wider reading. Areas for improvement included making the qualitative methodology clearer, explaining how synthetic data would be used ethically, and adding a project plan to demonstrate feasibility.

Artefact 2: Mini Research Project

The end-of-module assignment was a 3,000-word mini research project based on the same research topic as the proposal.

  • Due date: 15 December 2025
  • Weighting: 80%
  • Grade: 77% - Distinction
  • Focus: Annotated bibliography, methodology, data analysis, findings and recommendations

This project developed the initial proposal into a fuller research report. It included an annotated bibliography, discussion of methodology, analysis of findings, strengths and limitations, conclusion and recommendations.

Feedback praised the excellent knowledge and understanding of the topic, strong criticality in the annotated bibliography, use of thematic analysis, clear discussion of strengths and limitations, and strong overall structure. One suggested improvement was to include a clearer search strategy and use visualisation techniques such as a mind map or Venn diagram to make themes and findings more accessible.

Reflection and Professional Development

What I Learnt

This module improved my understanding of how research is planned, structured and communicated in computer science. I learnt how to form research aims, objectives and questions, carry out literature searches, compare academic sources and evaluate the strengths and limitations of existing research.

I also developed a stronger understanding of research ethics, especially in relation to sensitive data, cloud security and synthetic datasets. This helped me connect academic research with real-world concerns around privacy, generalisation, data protection and responsible technology use.

How I Responded to Feedback

After receiving feedback on the research proposal, I focused on improving the clarity of my methodology and developing a more detailed final project. In the end-of-module project, I strengthened the literature review, used thematic analysis and gave clearer discussion of strengths, limitations and findings.

Unit 9 Seminar: Professional Development and Future Directions

The Unit 9 seminar focused on career opportunities, professional portfolios, goal-setting and continuous professional development. This connected directly to the creation of this e-portfolio, as it encouraged me to document my achievements, reflect on my skills and plan future development.

Skills Gained

  • Academic research and literature searching
  • Critical reading and source evaluation
  • Research proposal writing
  • Annotated bibliography development
  • Ethical awareness and data protection understanding
  • Reflective writing and professional development planning
  • Academic referencing and structured report writing

Action Plan

  • Continue improving research methodology explanations in future academic work.
  • Use clearer search strategies when selecting academic sources.
  • Include more visual summaries such as mind maps, tables or diagrams where useful.
  • Continue building this e-portfolio as evidence of academic and technical development.
  • Connect research topics to my long-term interest in cloud, cybersecurity and SRE.
